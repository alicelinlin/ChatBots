Group Reflection

The results of this project lead one to conclude that teamwork is one of the most useful tools that can be used to enhance learning and promote critical thinking skills for all of us. This project gave us a very good opportunity to reflect on teamwork, such as the value of teamwork, the importance of organisation and structure when it comes to teamwork, and aspects relating to the contribution of individual members within our team and how it may affect the final project outcome.

Additionally, the results obtained from the interviews showed that reflection writing has several benefits in the classroom in terms of identifying the present state of knowledge gained in a particular course and help them become more self-independent learners. Interview results showed that the reflection was perceived as being a very beneficial tool for the participants in terms of identifying ones strengths and weaknesses (P3), or to improve and evaluate actions properly. Additionally, it was also reported that the reflection is also beneficial to examine students’ experiences and assess himself (P7). Most importantly, it is beneficial because it will help us correct our own mistakes and we will not repeat them in the future (P10).

Finally, Morzano’s Taxonomy showed that while writing a reflection students were able to express their attitudes, beliefs, and emotions about learning. Also, they could monitor and evaluate their progress throughout a course and comprehend, analyze and utilize new knowledge as it was presented above.

This was shown in the second interview, where the participant stated that “After several classes, I am now able to find meanings of some concepts and make connections with new information”. It is evident that the reflection helped this student to monitor and evaluate his own learning. Or, in example 3 “I use some learning strategies but there are so many new ones, for example the Key Word Method”, which shows that the student analyzed the use of his own learning strategies.

All in all, this study showed some interesting results on using a reflection paper as a self-assessment tool for learning EFL at a University level. We strongly believe that the results of the study will serve other educators and raise their awareness about its importance in education.

Justin Healy

Effective teamwork is critical to implementing and executing a common goal more quickly and efficiently in a team project. Each individual in our team possesses unique sets of abilities, weaknesses, strengths, and habits. As a result, it was critical to distribute the workload evenly according to these individual assets to ensure that everyone is satisfied and engaged in their roles.

During our first meeting, I divided the assignment into segments based on specific percentage values, and then each member selected their preferred section. However, once everyone began working, it became clear that this would not be easy, given how interconnected and dependent each area of the assignment was.

We lacked interdependence, and since everyone is working remote, it was difficult for us to create an effective group environment for all of us to work on one prototype; and since it’s the most busiest time of the whole semester, it became harder to get everyone together.

As a result, in the last couple of weeks, we determined that rather than concentrating exclusively on a single large prototype, it would probably be better to develop multiple prototypes, test them, and then evaluate the positives and negatives associated with each, just as we would in the real world.

One of our team's greatest accomplishments was the dedication of the majority of our members. While getting everyone together was not always easy, team meetings were always pleasant, and no team issues were ever discussed as problematic. Our team was constantly focused on the project and ways to improve the development process by examining tutor, client, and end user expectations.

Everyone was always willing to assist peers when they encountered difficulties with their sections, proactively brainstormed problems, offered assistance when necessary, and engaged in open dialogue that expressed concerns in an unaggressive manner.

However, as I previously stated in the profile section, unlike working on a real-world project, there was an absence of leadership. This resulted in difficulties in getting everyone together and lacking unity and consistency throughout the assignment, especially mid-way.

However, we resolved this issue by dividing the team into smaller subteams, each testing and working on different prototypes instead of working on one large prototype, with only a few members working on it. Therefore ultimately, our group achieved the goal of a cohesive team; despite dips in the middle, team cohesion was higher at the end of the semester than at the start.

Overall this team project turned out to be a very positive experience for me, and it a little sad that we've reached the end. Throughout the process, I've gained a firm grasp of my role's characteristics and learned how to maximize my strengths and abilities, also minimizing my weaknesses.

Successful teamwork relies on more than just one attribute but multiple, and it shows a compelling relationship between our team members who embraced these attributes. Although we are all year one IT students, the project was a success, and with additional time, I believe we can turn this into something tangible.

PLATFORMS USED TO BUILD OUR PRODUCT

Prototype Using Microsoft Azure

By Justin Healy


  • Atom was used along with Microsoft Azure to develop the prototype for this assignment.
  • Axios was used for the API requests on both the Front-end code, and server-side API calls. This is a great service. It makes API calls a much simpler process. It is a promise-based framework that is compatible with nearly all modern browsers. This is a significant advantage, as JavaScript has a native equivalent in the "fetch()" function. However, when we use it, we did run into compatibility issues. Particularly with older safari and internet explorer browsers. Despite the fact that this is only a prototype, the goal was to create a basic functional chat bot with scalability for future development. This is permitted by Axios.In terms of code, I used JavaScript, CSS and HTML to build the prototype.
  • In creating the above prototype, we used Microsoft Azure as the Engine, making use of their Cognitive services and QNA maker API. As well JavaScript, HTML, CSS in the front end and NodeJS to program a basic server.

We also took advantage of the cognitive service provided by Microsoft Azure to develop the prototype. The Microsoft Azure platform is difficult to use and very non-user friendly. But can be powerful when used correctly. The service used for the prototype was the QnA Maker API.

This is an interesting service from Microsoft as they make it possible to create a specific knowledge base from which business can use for customer enquiries. We used this service in order to create a Covid QnA Bot.

What it essentially does is allows the user to create an app from QnA maker portal then it creates an API from Microsoft's other services to serve as a QnA. Once you create a knowledge base with your custom FAQ, Azure will publish the service and give you an API address to call to. But what it does under the hood is compile a search service, an App Service, a cognitive service and a resource group. Then it uses all these when the QnA API is called to generate a response for the user.

Through the process of building the prototype the idea was to build something with a simple yet with scalable infrastructure to allow for future development. By having a backend this allows us to keep our API tokens secret and hide the logic from the end user. But the server is easily scalable by adding features to increase the Intelligence of the Bot. At the moment, the Bot receives a message from the user then send this message directly to the Microsoft API to be understood and generate a relevant response.

But further to this we could add some logic to the user’s question to decide whether the Microsoft API needs to be consulted. For example, we could store some questionand-answer combos for basic questions in the back end for quick use and consult the API for more complex questions.

My hope for this would be to one day phase out the use of any Intelligence framework and have a completely self-sustainable Machine learning and AI program to interact with our users without the added cost of external Frameworks.

The flow of the App runs like so:
  • The user clicks on the Bot Icon.
  • JavaScript is then used to render the chat window.
  • The user can then type a message and hit the send button.
  • The user’s message is then rendered in the chat window using JavaScript.
  • The message is then sent to the backend using Axios(API HTTP client for sending API requests)
  • The backend will then send the users question to Microsoft Azure.
  • A response is received from Microsoft Azure and sent back to the front end.
  • The front End then renders the response in the chat window.

    • And the process can continue like this indefinitely.
